Rules
  • On your turn, place the piece your opponent gave you.
  • After placing, choose one remaining piece for your opponent to place next.
  • Win by completing a row, column, or diagonal of four pieces that share one attribute.
  • Shared attributes are tall/short, light/dark, round/square, and solid/hole.
  • If all 16 pieces are placed with no shared-attribute line, the game is a draw.

Piece code: T/S = Tall/Short, L/D = Light/Dark, R/Q = Round/Square, F/H = Solid/Hole. Duplicate physical pieces are supported with #slot labels. Squares: A1-D4.

    About Quarto

    Quarto is an abstract strategy board game played on a 4 by 4 grid with 16 unique pieces. Each piece has four attributes: tall or short, light or dark, round or square, and solid or hollow.

    The twist is that you do not choose the piece you place. Your opponent hands you a piece, you place it on the board, and then you choose the next piece for them. The goal is to be the first player to make a line of four pieces that share at least one attribute.
